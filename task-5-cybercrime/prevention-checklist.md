1. The "No-PIN" Golden Rule (UPI): Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money. If a "buyer" on OLX or a stranger asks you to enter your PIN to "verify" a payment into your account, it is 100% a scam.

2. Verify the VPA (Financial): Before clicking "Pay" on any UPI app, double-check the recipient's name (VPA). Scammers often place fake QR stickers over real ones at local shops or send "Collect Requests" that look like "Payment Received" alerts.

3. Master the Manager: Stop using the same password for your VIT student portal, Gmail, and Instagram. Use a Password Manager (like Bitwarden) to create and store long, unique passphrases for every single site.

4. Lock the Front Door (2FA): Enable Two-Factor Authentication on your primary email and social accounts. Even if a hacker steals your password, they can't get in without the secondary code from your phone.

5. Public Wi-Fi = Danger Zone: Avoid logging into your bank or student accounts on free campus or cafe Wi-Fi. If you must use it, always turn on a VPN to encrypt your data from "sniffing" attacks.

6. The "Grandmother" Test: Before posting a photo or a "venting" rant on social media, ask: "Would I be okay with a future employer or my professor seeing this?" If not, it stays in the drafts. Your digital footprint is permanent.

7. Spot the Phish: Be wary of "Urgent" emails claiming your account will be suspended or offering "Instant Internships." Always check the sender's actual email address; if it looks like support@v1t-bhopal.com (with a '1' instead of an 'i'), it’s a trap.

8. Report Instantly: If you are targeted, don't stay silent. Call the National Cybercrime Helpline at 1930 immediately and file a report at cybercrime.gov.in.
